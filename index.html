About Me

I am a final year PhD candidate in Data Analytics and Machine Learning group at TUM. I have 7+ years of research experience in ML and Vision along with publications in top CV conferences and 5+ years of experience of applying safe AI-based solutions to industrial applications and use-cases at Fraunhofer IKS, Munich. My research topics include:

  • Interpretable Out-of-Distribution Detection or Outlier Detection using Vision models
  • Novel Object Detection and Segmentation in images and videos
  • Inherently Human - Interpretable models based on global concepts and prototypes, Explainable AI
  • Reliable person and anomaly detection around cageless robots
  • LLM and Multimodal GenAI applications in industrial automation use-cases

Professional Experience

  • Research Engineer, Fraunhofer IKS, Munich
    2019 - Present
    As a part of AI team, developed and implemented multiple industrial projects and research use-cases:
    • Published first zero-shot method PROWL for large obstacle detector on train track using vision foundation models in safe.trAIn project
    • Published first open vocabulary OOD object detector in Automated Driving using multi-modal VLM
    • Innovating solutions using foundation model detectors in real-time inference over video for dependable person detection use-case around cageless robots
    • Published and clinically validated ProtoVerse model for enhancing interpretability of AI-assisted medical use-case (Vertebrae Fracture Grading) using human-interpretable prototypes
    • Conducted research on Safe AI topics such as robustness, uncertainty quantification, Out-of-Distribution Detction, Anomaly Detection, Interpretability] through diverse projects
  • Research Intern, Sony Europe Limited, Stuttgart
    2018
    As part of Computational Imaging Group, conducted research on Semi-supervised learning with auto-encoder for material (skin vs non-skin) classification and outlier removal from limited multi-spectral images.
